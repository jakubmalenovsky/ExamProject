This scenic route takes you from Horní Bečva to Rožnov pod Radhoštěm, passing through Čertův Kámen and the iconic Radhošť. The trail offers stunning views of the mountains, dense forests, and peaceful meadows. While the route is long, it can be shortened, for example, by taking a ride up to Pustevny, a popular destination in the area. The trail is moderately challenging with gradual ascents and clear markings, making it suitable for both experienced hikers and families seeking an adventure. Along the way, you'll find rest spots and opportunities to admire the breathtaking natural beauty, including the famous Radhošť Chapel and the legendary Čertův Kámen. A memorable journey through the heart of the Beskydy mountains.

Restaurants

For a pleasant break along the route, there are several dining options to consider. Martinák restaurant in Horní Bečva offers a cozy atmosphere and traditional Czech dishes, perfect for fueling up before starting your hike. As you approach Pustevny, you’ll find various small cafés and eateries catering to hikers and visitors. At the top of Radhošť, you can stop at the Radegast Hotel and Restaurant. This restaurant offers a great place to relax, enjoy a hearty meal, and take in the stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Transport

To reach Horní Bečva from Rožnov pod Radhoštěm, you can take a local bus, which provides a convenient connection between the two locations. The bus ride offers a scenic journey and is the easiest way to reach the starting point of the hike. If you wish to shorten the hike, you can take a bus or car to Pustevny, a popular tourist destination that can be easily accessed from both Rožnov and Horní Bečva. From Pustevny, you can enjoy the stunning views and start your hike at a higher elevation, reducing the overall distance and time needed for the trek.

Equipment

For this hike, you'll need basic outdoor gear suitable for a day of walking through mountain terrain. Comfortable, sturdy walking shoes or light hiking boots are essential, as the trail can be uneven in some sections. A small backpack is recommended to carry water, snacks, and any personal items. Depending on the weather, bring layers to stay warm or cool, such as a light jacket or a sweater. Don't forget sunscreen, a hat, and sunglasses for sun protection. If you're hiking during the cooler months, consider packing a rain jacket or poncho, as the weather in the mountains can be unpredictable. In really deep winter, it’s also advisable to bring anti-slip spikes to ensure safety on icy or snowy patches.
